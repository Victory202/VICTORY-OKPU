WHO Dashboard Overview

  • Tools Used: Power BI and Microsoft Excel
  • Github URL: Project Link

This Global Health Dashboard, designed with Power BI, provides a comprehensive view of health performance and infrastructure, focusing on regional or national development through a World Health Organization (WHO) perspective.

Core health indicators highlight the baseline health of the population, including life expectancy of 63.4 years, infant mortality of 61.4 deaths per 1,000 live births, and health expenditure accounting for 4.08% of GDP.

The dashboard monitors disease prevalence and medical infrastructure distribution. Vaccine distribution is tracked across two tiers (3.51K and 7.02K units), while hospital bed deployment shows yearly trends: 2.2K beds in 2020, 4.5K in 2021, and 3.1K in 2022.

Disease prevalence analysis covers major health threats, including COVID-19, Malaria, HIV/AIDS, and Tuberculosis, enabling stakeholders to understand the impact and prioritize interventions effectively.

Visual analytics provide deeper insights, including a scatter plot examining the relationship between health spending and life expectancy, and geographic maps identifying critical hotspots requiring focused healthcare resources.

Overall, this dashboard transforms complex health and infrastructure data into actionable insights, supporting decision-making, policy planning, and strategic allocation of medical resources to improve public health outcomes.
